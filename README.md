# islah-ai-transparency-standard
🕊️ ISLAH AI TRANSPARENCY STANDARD (IATS) — v1.0 DRAFT
Purpose

The Islah AI Transparency Standard (IATS) provides a neutral, human-centered framework for clearly identifying content created or assisted by artificial intelligence across media formats.

Its goal is to preserve trust, informed consent, and media clarity in the digital age without stigmatizing responsible AI use.

Core Principle

No person should have to guess whether they are interacting with AI-generated or AI-assisted content.

Transparency supports both human dignity and technological progress.

Content Classification
🟢 HUMAN

Content created primarily by humans without substantive AI generation.

🟡 AI-ASSISTED

AI tools used for drafting, editing, enhancement, or support, with meaningful human oversight and responsibility.

🔵 AI-GENERATED

Content substantially produced by AI systems, including text, images, video, audio, or synthetic media.

🔴 SYNTHETIC IDENTITY CONTENT

AI-generated representations of real or fictional persons, including synthetic voice, likeness, or behavior.

Disclosure Guidelines

Appropriate labeling should be:

Clear

Neutral in tone

Visible or audible

Non-deceptive

Context-appropriate

Media Implementation Examples

Images: Visible badge and/or metadata tag
Video: On-screen indicator or description note
Audio / Radio: Verbal disclosure
Interactive Systems: Introductory statement of AI involvement

Guiding Values

Human-centered design

Neutrality

Accessibility

Cross-cultural clarity

Non-punitive transparency

Support for responsible innovation

Status

This document represents an open proposal intended for voluntary adoption, discussion, and improvement.

It does not replace applicable laws or regulations.

🌐 Origin

Proposed by the Islah Nexus initiative as part of a broader effort to support clarity, trust, and responsible use of artificial intelligence.
